Slide 3 of 5 · Core Tension

Today vs. Tomorrow

The fundamental design challenge: preserving trust while enabling exploration

What We Value Today → What We Want to Enable
Consistency & control via pre-agreed tables ⇄ Flexibility & free exploration
Static dashboards — pull when needed ⇄ Proactive, pushed insight notifications
Tables that show everything, reconcile cleanly ⇄ Patterns, trends & forward-looking signals
Standard views for governance ⇄ Personalised, actionable discovery
Structured drilldowns within rows/columns ⇄ Natural language / voice intent queries

✅ Jarvis must make both sides coexist — not choose one over the other

Slide 4 of 5 · Challenges

Hard Problems to Crack

Technical and design challenges the team must tackle

🔍

Root Cause, Not Surface Noise

AI must explain why a variance happened — linking financial outcomes to commercial drivers — not just describe what the number is.

🏗️

Hierarchy Integrity at Scale

Brand→TA→Total AZ, Market→Region→Total AZ and Sub-unit→Unit→Total AZ must remain consistent across every view, drill and AI response.

🔒

Fine-Grained Access Control

CEO sees everything; a market lead sees only their market. RBAC must be enforced at query level, not just the UI — including AI-generated answers.

⚖️

Fact vs. AI Interpretation

Every AI-generated insight must be clearly labelled: is it a confirmed data fact, a model interpretation, or a hypothesis? Trust depends on this boundary.

📱

Mobile-First, Table-First Culture

Users expect reconciled totals and full-hierarchy tables. The mobile UX must preserve that trust while not becoming a cramped spreadsheet.

🔔

Push Without Alert Fatigue

Deciding which variances are worth surfacing proactively — and when — without overwhelming leaders with irrelevant noise.
