AI30 Data to Insights Hackathon 
Hackathon to reimagine the future enterprise-grade analytics platform 
Executive Summary 
We are evolving toward a future where trusted, reconciled data can be consumed in multiple ways — tables, visuals, natural language queries, voice queries, or intent-driven interaction and where AI can significantly augment analytical thinking, find trends and elevate decisions.   
We want to empower employees across all functions with timely, relevant and value-add insights and information, that enable faster, more robust decision-making, without sacrificing rigor, ultimately to drive business value. 
This Hackathon challenge is to create a bold and credible product concept and prototype that delivers: 
A seamless balance between standardisation and personalisation flexibility 
Clarity on true root causes of performance, not surface level fluctuations 
Coexistence between today’s strengths (control through pre-agreed tables, consistency of views, pull) and tomorrow’s needs (flexibility, exploration, push) 
AI-based augmentation: detecting variances, identifying trends, guiding navigation, and maintaining clear boundaries between facts, interpretations, and hypotheses based augmentation 
Success means creating solutions that uphold trust and transparency, evolve naturally from our table-led culture, and demonstrate how AI can responsibly scale insight discovery across the enterprise to drive high quality business decisions.

1. Overview 
We are inviting teams across AZ to participate in a hackathon focused on reimagining the future user experience of an enterprise-wide data and insight platform.  
Our organisation manages complex, global data across all functions — including finance, commercial, operations, R&D, and enabling units. 
Today, this data is primarily consumed through standardized, table reports and semi-static dashboards (eg table with ability to drill within a row/column and with ability to tailor via filters). These systems provide rigor, trust, and consistency but limit flexibility in discovery. Excitingly teams are also embracing new technologies – using AI enables tools to predict next best actions based on data within AZ Brain, and using AI for insights for International region using Symphony AI and enterprise wide in AZpire, to name but a few.  
The goal of this hackathon is to build on our work to date.  We want to re-imagine what a credible, human-centered evolution could look like by 2028, building on today’s strengths while unlocking new ways of consuming and using insights. We seek bold and credible concepts that reflect our entrepreneurial culture and push us forward. And could be implemented for some areas as early as 2026.

4. Our Current State (Important Cultural Context) 
We are a highly table-driven organization with strong reliance on predefined reports, static dashboards, and structured drilldowns. People trust tables because they show everything, reconcile cleanly, reduce narrative bias and “cherry picking”, and reinforce shared governance. They also enable a standardise hierarchy.  
This cultural strength, however, creates friction for users who prefer different ways of consuming information or exploring insights.

6. User Experience Questions to Explore 
A. Modes of Consumption 
How do users choose their preferred mode — table, visual, question prompt, chat?  
Can they switch seamlessly between table ↔ chart and summary ↔ detail and drill down without losing context? 
B. Preset vs Personalised 
What must remain standardised? (eg hierarchies are a must have) 
Where does personalisation add real value? 
How do we avoid bespoke chaos while enabling flexibility? 
C. Explaining Variance and Performance 
How do we help users uncover the underlying business reason behind a variance not just descriptive commentary?  
How can explanations be best visualized alongside data? 
D. Access security 
The solution must enable to grant different access to data to different people (eg CEO can see everything while somebody in a market can only see data from such market).
